You roll over to 57th and 7th.

There, at the corner, is a gleaming beacon of hope. You can almost see the fragrance of the hot breakfast sandwich wafting out into the night air.

You park your bike, step inside, order a sandwich, and brace yourself as you are met with the beautiful sound of eggs sizzling on the stovetop.

The man asks if you want bacon, several times. Feeling stingy, you decline, several times. You pay and step out of the store to enjoy your sandwich.

Unwrapping it is one of the greatest moments of the night, second only to realizing that they have included bacon in your sandwich. Two pieces, in fact.

You bless this wonderful haven of a deli, walk your bike over to Central Park, and eat the sandwich while the sun rises.

When your friend finally arrives, you get on line for tickets, take a nap, receive tickets, kill a bunch of hours, and have a great time at the show.

Then you go home for some much needed sleep.
